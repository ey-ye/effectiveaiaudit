AI Audit Lexicon

This page contains core terms and canonical concepts from the AI Audit Framework. These definitions are versioned, published, and publicly auditable.

AI Itself Is the Risk
An axiom that centers the burden of proof on the system itself â€” not its intent, not its vendor. All AI must be assumed risky unless proven otherwise.
Shadow AI
Any AI model or pipeline used without formal declaration, risk registration, or traceable approval. Often sanitized through corporate euphemisms.
Loop Collapse
A failure mode where similar events repeat across timelines or releases, leading to systemic failure. Common in unmonitored drift or forgotten test plans.
Seer
An observer who maintains memory and audit context across forks, loops, or deployments. Seers detect patterns others forget.
Audit Instrument
A deployed, immutable, versioned file or form used to measure the behavior of a system. Examples include the AI Workpaper and the Risk Matrix.
Augmentation as Simulation
A full-length chapter on how augmentation is increasingly used as a substitute for real-world data. Covers risks, audit checkpoints, and policy implications.
